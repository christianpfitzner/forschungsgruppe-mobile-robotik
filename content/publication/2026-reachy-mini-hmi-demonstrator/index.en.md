abstract: |
  The Technology Transfer Centre (TTZ) of TH Nürnberg has acquired a Reachy Mini humanoid
  desktop robot by Pollen Robotics. The robot is to be operated as an interactive demonstrator
  for Human-Machine Interaction (HMI) in the TTZ entrance area, giving visitors a tangible
  experience of the possibilities offered by modern human-robot interaction.

  The goal of this thesis is the complete commissioning of the Reachy Mini and the development
  of one or more HMI demonstration applications that can be operated intuitively without any
  technical background. This includes setting up the system software, integrating perception
  components (e.g. face recognition, voice control), and implementing an autonomous interaction
  scenario that makes the robot feel alive and welcoming to visitors.

  On the software side, a robust, maintainable architecture based on ROS 2 is to be developed.
  Interfaces for gesture recognition, speech input and output, and simple animation sequences
  are to be implemented and integrated into a coherent system. Finally, operating instructions
  and a concept for continuous operation are to be prepared.

## Motivation

Robots have long since moved out of factory floors and into everyday life — yet many people
still feel a certain reluctance towards human-robot interaction. The Reachy Mini, with its
expressive humanoid design, offers a unique opportunity to lower this threshold in the TTZ
entrance area: visitors should be able to experience, playfully and without any prior
knowledge, how intuitively modern HMI concepts can work.

## Work Packages

**Commissioning & System Integration**
- Setting up the system software (operating system, ROS 2, Pollen Robotics vendor software)
- Calibration of motors, camera, and microphones
- Networking and integration into the TTZ IT infrastructure

**Perception & Interaction**
- Integration of face recognition and gaze-tracking (e.g. OpenCV, MediaPipe)
- Speech input and output (e.g. Whisper, text-to-speech)
- Development of animation sequences and gestures for expressive behaviour

**Demonstration Application**
- Design and implementation of one or more HMI scenarios (e.g. greeting ritual,
  guided interaction, reactive behaviour triggered by proximity)
- User interface for easy start/stop of the demo by non-technical staff

**Continuous Operation & Documentation**
- Development of a robust start/stop concept for unattended operation
- Preparation of an operating manual and technical documentation
- Safety concept for public operation in the entrance area

## Requirements

- Knowledge of Python and ROS 2
- Interest in human-robot interaction and software development
- Ideally experience with computer vision or speech processing
- Enthusiasm for creative design of interaction scenarios
- Ability to work independently and communicate effectively
